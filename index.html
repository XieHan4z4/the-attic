FIG. 01 // CORE CONCEPT

"Memory is a dusty attic where clarity must be earned through stillness."

The Attic is an immersive sound odyssey that physicalizes the fragmented nature of human recollection. In this near-pitch-black void, participants must navigate through auditory proximity. Adult restlessness is manifested as chaotic static noise, which only subsides when the participant chooses to stand still—allowing the delicate echoes of childhood memories to emerge from the silence.

FIG. 02 // RESEARCH & CONTEXT

The Subconscious Repository

Inspired by John Cage and the concept of Sensory Deprivation, our work explores the attic as a quintessentially psychological space. As the highest and most neglected part of a house, it serves as a metaphor for the deep subconscious. By enforcing visual restraint, we shift the hierarchy of senses, rewarding the participant's auditory focus with clarity that vision can no longer provide.

FIG. 06 // POST-PROJECT REFLECTION

The Attic stands as a rebellion against the ocular-centric nature of modern interaction. By removing the safety of vision, we invited participants to "listen" to their own past. The project confirms that emotional immersion is most powerful when it requires the user's active, patient participation.
