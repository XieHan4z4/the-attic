The Attic

XIE Han (59938176)  |  Wong Sze Yui (56286397)  |  YIN Yihe (59968783)
"The Attic" is an immersive sound experience that mirrors the fragmented nature of human memory. In a world of perpetual motion, our past often becomes a hazy cacophony. Here, memory is literalized as a dimly lit attic—a space where clarity is not given, but earned through stillness and proximity.

Movement generates restlessness (static noise), while silence and careful approach allow the "echoes" of childhood—an old music box, a toy train—to emerge from the darkness.

0. Project Demonstration

1. Background & Inspiration

Our research for The Attic spanned three interconnected dimensions: the philosophy of noise, the psychology of memory spaces, and the mechanics of sensory deprivation.

The Relativity of Noise and Silence

Inspired by John Cage, we view silence not as the absence of sound, but as a state of heightened receptivity. In modern life, "internal noise" (anxiety and pace) drowns out our core memories. We translated this into a game mechanic: player movement triggers a global distortion, representing the chaotic pace of adulthood. Stillness, therefore, becomes the "key" to filtering out the noise of the present to hear the whispers of the past.

The Attic as a Psychological Repository

The attic is the quintessential metaphor for the subconscious—a dusty, neglected space at the top of the house where the past is stored. By placing the experience in a near-pitch-black attic, we simulate the effort required to recall a specific moment. The volume attenuation based on distance mimics the psychological process of a memory shifting from a blurred impression to a vivid recollection.

Sensory Deprivation & Blind Navigation

Drawing from pure-audio experiences like Blindscape, we implemented extreme visual restraint. By removing conventional lighting, we force the participant to abandon their ocular-centric habits and rely entirely on an "auditory radar." This sensory shift amplifies the emotional impact of finally "finding" a sound in the void.

2. Scene & Experience Design

We followed a philosophy of "Minimalism and Sensory Deprivation" across both virtual and physical environments to ensure a unified experience.

Virtual Environment: Edge-based Navigation

Virtual Layout: 10 memory objects distributed along the boundaries of the attic.

To assist players in a visually pitch-black environment, we utilized an "edge-based" layout. By placing all 10 objects along the walls, the physical boundary of the room acts as a guide. When players feel the "collision" in the dark, they can naturally follow the wall to discover the hidden audio fragments.

Physical Exhibition: Atmospheric Synergy

Exhibition Site: Utilizing dual-channel speakers and a projector in a darkened room.

The physical exhibition utilized a projector, dual speakers, and a controller. To preserve the immersion, the physical room was kept as dark as possible. This created a synergy where the boundaries between the physical room and the virtual attic blurred. Furthermore, we customized the controller response curve in the engine, requiring participants to push the joystick with extreme delicacy to move, mirroring the psychological caution of navigating a dark, fragile memory.

3. Sound Design & Audio Processing

We curated 10 nostalgic objects and 1 ambient background layer to construct the sonic world. All assets (sourced from Freesound and Pixabay) were heavily re-processed in Audacity to achieve a "muffled, hazy, and distant" timbre.

Audio post-processing: Using Low-Pass filters and Reverb to create a vintage aesthetic.

4. Technical Implementation

The system utilizes a dual-engine architecture: Unreal Engine 5 for spatial data and Max/MSP for algorithmic mixing, bridged via the OSC (Open Sound Control) protocol.

UE5: Spatial Tracking & Visual Feedback

UE5 Blueprint: Calculating player velocity and object distances for OSC output.

We calculated the distance between the player and the 10 objects in real-time, alongside the player's velocity. These values were packaged as OSC messages and sent to Max/MSP via local port 8000.

Visual Feedback: Mapping distance to Point Light intensity via "Map Range Clamped."

Max/MSP: Algorithmic Mixing & Modulation

Max/MSP: Data routing and volume attenuation based on spatial proximity.

Background Suppression: Using zl.sort, Max/MSP identifies the closest object. As the player approaches, the system attenuates the global background noise to create a "clarity effect" for the specific memory.

Speed-Driven Modulation: Mapping velocity to metallic distortion and gain.

The core artistic feedback is the Speed-Driven Modulation. High velocity values trigger aggressive algorithmic modulation, creating a chaotic, metallic noise that instinctively encourages players to slow down or stand still.

5. Reflection & References

Subverting the Visual Hierarchy

The Attic was a deliberate rebellion against traditional game design, which often treats sound as a secondary garnish to visuals. We asked: Can we build narrative through sound alone? By enforcing visual deprivation and mapping movement to "noise," we successfully shifted the focus to the auditory realm. The feedback from participants confirmed that the "relief" of finding a clear sound in the void is a powerful emotive force that high-definition graphics cannot replicate.
